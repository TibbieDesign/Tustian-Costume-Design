The art of costume design involves part historical research, part understanding the needs of the characters in the play, and part imagination. A good costume can bring an actor confidence, and inform their performance choices. By incorporating pieces that impact body movement, you can help the actor physically inhabit their role.

You must be able to work as part of a team to bring the vision of the director to life. Your costumes must work well with the set design and the lighting design. I have been very lucky to work with some incredibly talented directors, actors, set, lighting, hair, and make-up designers. It is truly a joy when it all comes together to create theater magic.
